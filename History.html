History of pizza

"Pizza's Global Spread"

Pizza's popularity spread beyond Naples and throughout Italy in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. It became a staple food for all classes, enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, and dinner.

- Italian immigrants brought pizza to America, where it initially remained popular in Italian neighborhoods.
- After World War II, American soldiers stationed in Italy introduced pizza to a wider audience, leading to its rapid rise in popularity in the United States
THE MAIN BRIEF EXPLANATION: The main explanation of the section "Pizza's Global Spread" is that pizza originated in Naples, Italy, and spread across Italy in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. It became popular in Italian-American communities after being introduced to the U.S. by immigrants.
